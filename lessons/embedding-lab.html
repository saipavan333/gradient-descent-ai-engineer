Where do these vectors come from? Real embeddings aren't hand-placed — a model learns them by predicting which words appear together, then a technique like PCA or t-SNE squashes the hundreds of real dimensions down to two so we can look. But the property is genuine: distance means similarity, and direction means relationship. That's what powers semantic search, recommendations, and RAG.

Try these

1. Find neighbours. In Explore mode, click king. Its closest words are other royalty and male terms — because the model placed related meanings nearby. That "nearby = similar" is the entire basis of vector search.

2. Run the classic analogy. Switch to Analogy and pick king − man + woman. The grey arrow (man→king) and the blue arrow (woman→result) are parallel and the same length — so the result lands exactly on queen.

3. Prove it's a direction, not magic. Try uncle − man + woman and actor − man + woman. The same "male→female" arrow works everywhere, giving aunt and actress. One consistent direction, reused.

What this lab proves
  • Distance = similarity. Nearby vectors mean related meanings — the engine behind semantic search and RAG.
  • Direction = relationship. A consistent offset (male→female, singular→plural) is reused across the whole space.
  • Analogies are vector arithmetic. A − B + C lands on the word that completes the relationship.
  • Real embeddings are just higher-dimensional. Same idea, hundreds of dimensions, learned from data.
