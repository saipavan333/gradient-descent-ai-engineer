Decoding & Sampling Lab

A language model doesn't output words — it outputs a probability for every possible next word. How you pick from that distribution decides everything: whether the model is boring and factual or wild and creative. These are the knobs behind every "temperature" setting you've ever seen.

Here's the model's next-token distribution for one prompt. Turn temperature up to flatten it (more random), down to sharpen it (more deterministic). Then use top-k and top-p to chop off the unlikely tail so the model can be creative without going off the rails. Sample a token and watch it come from exactly these bars.

The pipeline, in order. (1) Divide the scores by temperature and softmax them into probabilities. (2) top-k keeps only the k most likely tokens. (3) top-p keeps the smallest set of tokens whose probability adds up to p (the "nucleus"). (4) Renormalize what's left and sample. Greedy skips all of it and just takes the top token every time — repeatable, but often dull and repetitive.

Try these

1. Temperature extremes. Slide temperature to 0.1 — one bar dominates, the model is nearly deterministic. Slide to 2.0 — the bars flatten out and even unlikely words get a real chance. This single number is the creativity dial.

2. Cut the tail with top-p. Set top-p to 0.9. The least likely words are struck through — they can no longer be sampled, so the model stays coherent even at higher temperature. This is why nucleus sampling is the modern default.

3. Greedy vs sampling. Turn on greedy and press Generate — every sample is identical. Turn it off and generate again — you get variety. That's the difference between a factual assistant and a creative writer.

What this lab proves
  • Generation = sampling from a distribution. The model gives probabilities; decoding picks a token.
  • Temperature reshapes the distribution. Low = sharp/deterministic, high = flat/creative.
  • top-k and top-p trim the tail. They remove implausible tokens so higher temperature stays coherent.
  • Greedy is repeatable but repetitive. Always-take-the-top loses all diversity.
