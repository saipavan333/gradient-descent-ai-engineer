Attention Lab

The one mechanism behind every modern language model: each word looks at every other word and decides how much to borrow from it. Here it's computed live — real scaled dot-product attention, softmax(QKᵀ/√d) — and drawn as arcs you can read.

Click a word to see what it pays attention to. Then switch heads: watch the same sentence light up completely differently — one head links a pronoun to its noun, another links a verb to its subject. That specialization, discovered by training, is why transformers understand structure.

▶ Self-attention, visualized

Click any word (or a row in the grid) to make it the query. Arc thickness = how much attention it sends to each other word. The grid is the full attention matrix.

Attention head — each was “trained” to track a different relationship
i
What are Q, K and V? Every token is projected into a query ("what am I looking for?"), a key ("what do I offer?") and a value ("what will I hand over?"). A token's query is compared to every key by dot product; softmax turns those scores into weights; the output is the weighted mix of values. Different heads use different projections — so they ask different questions of the same sentence.

Try these

1. Follow a pronoun. On the coreference head, click it. The thickest arc lands on cat — the model has linked the pronoun to what it refers to, across four words. That long-range link is exactly what older models (RNNs) struggled with.

2. Find the subject. Switch to the verb→subject head and click sat. Now the attention flows to cat — the thing doing the sitting. Same sentence, different question.

3. Turn on the causal mask. Now each word can only attend to itself and earlier words — the upper triangle goes dark. This is the difference between a model that reads the whole sentence at once (BERT-style) and one that generates left-to-right (GPT-style).

4. Sharpen the focus. Drop the temperature: attention concentrates on a single word (confident). Raise it: attention spreads out (hedging). It's the same knob that controls randomness when a model generates text.

What this lab proves
  • Attention is a soft lookup — a query scores every key, softmax makes weights, the output is a weighted blend of values.
  • Range is free — a word can attend to any other word directly, near or far, in one step. No recurrence, no forgetting.
  • Heads specialize — with different Q/K projections, different heads track different relationships (coreference, syntax, position) in parallel.
  • The causal mask makes a generator — hide the future and the same machinery becomes a left-to-right predictor like GPT.
